# Write Brief

Produce a tight one-pager through a fast, focused gate process.

<HARD-GATE>
Do NOT write the brief until these three things are confirmed:
1. The problem or opportunity
2. The ask (what you want the reader to do or approve)
3. What success looks like
</HARD-GATE>

## Process

### Gate 1: The problem or opportunity

Ask: "What's the problem or opportunity this brief is about? One or two sentences."

Reflect back and confirm.

### Gate 2: The ask

Ask: "What are you asking for? (e.g., headcount, budget, approval to proceed, a decision, alignment on direction)"

Reflect back and confirm.

### Gate 3: Success criteria

Ask: "What does success look like if this is approved and executed?"

Reflect back and confirm.

### Write the brief

```markdown
# [Initiative Name]

**Date:** YYYY-MM-DD
**Author:** [if known]
**Status:** Draft

## Problem / Opportunity

[1-3 sentences. What's happening, why it matters, why now.]

## Proposal

[What you're proposing to do. 2-4 sentences.]

## Ask

[Exactly what you need from the reader — decision, resource, approval.]

## Success Criteria

- [Criterion 1]
- [Criterion 2]

## Risks / Tradeoffs

- [Key risk or tradeoff — be honest]

## Not In Scope

- [What this brief explicitly does not cover]
```

Save to: `docs/pm/briefs/YYYY-MM-DD-[topic]-brief.md`
